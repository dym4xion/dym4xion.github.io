W017: Lina Wertmüller

Week starting: 2021-01-11

The Seduction of Mimi (1972)

Mimi has a real respect for dialogue but can still do a lot even in it's less dialogue heavy scenes. It's more lighthearted in tone rather than consistently laugh out loud funny but it does have one or two particularly outrageous moments.

It's hard to not be constantly aware of how Italian it is (maybe this is part of the satire) especially when it has a scene composed almost entirely of hand gestures.

It's arguably very out of date on certain topics, but why wouldn't it be? It features a one particularly brutal scene of spousal abuse that is so horiffic that I feel safe in assuming that it must be some sort of commentary.

Love & Anarchy (1973)

Giannini, Melato and Fiore are just as entertaining here as they were in Mimi but Lina Polito also shines in her role as Tripolina. It can be quite meandering but the meandering is fun and the third act redeems any slowness felt in the first two.

Seven Beauties (1975)

Giancarlo Giannini is more isolated in this story in a sense but he has enough range to hold the focus that the film wants to maintain on Pasqualino. What is sorely missed is a more robust counter to his character that we get from Mariangela Melato in the previous two films. The upporting cast is good though especially Elena Fiore who, as far as I can tell, is the only other person to feature in all three films along G.G.. Ultimately, though, the disjuncted nature of the film prohibits any real intimate look at a supporting character which would in turn tell us more about Giannini's character.

The war montage intro is pretty interesting and does a good job of setting the tone for the rest of the film which features some brutal concentration camp scenes interspersed with scenes of the main character before he was captured such that its non-linear telling eventually gives the whole story. Perhaps in this respect the film works best as a dramatic character study of Pasqualino with some comedic elements rather than an outright comedy. It can feel like it escapes categorisation but maybe it perfectly fits the category of 'black comedy' and that maybe there are components of the film that can't be properly translated through subtitles. Maybe there is a greater point to be made about how tragedy is somehow more univeral than comedy as there are some really instensly tragic scenes in this film, particularly towards the end.

An interesting unintended coincidence to note is how similar it ends to the ending of last weeks final film Ascent.

Notes and Conclusions

Three overtly political comedies(?) mostly centering around the themes of anarchism and fascism in Italy. Wertmüller does a good job of getting good performances out of her cast and the direction generally is inventive and works well to keep the story moving throughout all three. My favourite would probably be Love & Anrachy which is probably why I have so little to say about it. Mimi is fun too, but I would have a hard time fully recommending Seven Beauties; not because it isn't good (it is) but purely out of how uncomfortable it can be to watch I can understand that many may not enjoy watching it, but it is provoking nonetheless.
