W021: 3 Remaining Ozu in Colour

Week starting: 2021-02-12

Equinox Flower (1958)

"In the dark we used to think we might all die together."

Late Autumn (1960)

Interesting to see Hara as the Mother figure in this one as opposed to what she played in previous Ozu films. This is handled well and serves as a different iteration on shared themes and concepts covered in Ozu's films.

The End of Summer (1961)

Nakamura is great to watch as the old owner of a sake brewing company juggling life between his two families.

Notes and Conclusions

I think this is it, for now. No more Ozu films for a while. I've enjoyed them all quite a bit but it has got to the point now where they are becoming a bit hard to distiguish and that some of them have started to merge into one sort of collage of match-making, weddings, sake drinking, reflecting on the war, dinner table discussions and lonely parents. It may have been a mistake to watch so many of them back-to-back, but I was uniquely charmed by the warmth and style of these films which was hard to forget when trying to choose the films for each successive 'next week'. It might be worth mentioning that this consistency of style and content could also be in-part due to not just Ozu but the returning crew with Yuharu Atsuta's cinematography and Kogo Noda's co-writing as well as several returning cast members.

There are still many Ozu films I haven't seen (especially some of his earlier work) so I anticipate eventually returning to his work. What this three week experiment has given me, though, is an opportunity to think about what stands out as more memorable than others. In this regard it is easy to see why Tokyo Story and Late Spring are so widely celebrated as they really do stick in the mind, but I would also throw into the mix Good Morning as one that is perhaps a bit undersung.
