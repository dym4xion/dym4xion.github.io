W022: Hitchcock's Spies

Week starting: 2021-MM-DD

The Man Who Knew Too Much (1934)

The 39 Steps (1935)

Secret Agent (1936)

Notes and Conclusions

The first two follow a sort of wrong man character caught up in a situation more thrilling than what they are used to, whereas the seccond is a more conventional Spy vs. Spy thriller. Peter Lorre is particularly charismatic in Secret Agent and TMWKTM, however The 39 Steps stands out as the better film and I found it to be the most engaging out of the three. What doesn't go unnoticed in these three is how modern they feel, and this makes me wonder whether Hitchcock was ahead of his time or moreso just highly influential. Who can say for sure?
