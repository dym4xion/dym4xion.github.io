W015: Věra Chytilová

Week starting: 2020-12-28

Something Different (1963)

A wonderfully simple slice-of-life contrasting the lived experiences of a small family and the difficulties that come with marriage and parenthood against a professional gymnast as she trains for her final competition. In both stories there is a searching for (you guessed it) something different in their every-day lives.

I think the gymnasts training is the highlight for me. it's fascinating to watch and gives such weight to how the competition is experienced too. The home-setting scenes are good too and contain what you would likely expect from a social realist picture: children messing around; cooking and cleaning; feeding pets; bedtime stories; forgetting why you walkd into a room; affairs; habitual boredom; straightening picture frames.

Daisies (1966)

Because it's so outwardly silly, Daisies may be one of the most accesssible experimental films I have yet to see. It's near total irreverance, however, doesn't lessen to any extent the quite serious statement it contains - rather you get to enjoy what it has to say in this altogether freaky, quirky, and often hilarious trip of a film proporting it's fairly cogent anti-war message.

Panelstory (1980)

Continuing the slice-of-life social realism of Something Different, Panelstory looks closer (as it's full title would suggest) at the creation of a new residential apartment block area and the communities that spring from that. It's greatest merit is how transportive it is. In the way we see the characters interact with each other and their surroundings it's easy to feel as if you are experiencing common everyday life in socialist Czechoslovakia is such a way more immersive than what you would be able to attain just by reading its history. Whether or not this is an authentic portrayal, I have no idea, but I assume that it can't be too far off as it doesn't overly romanticise the experience and well addresses lacking healthcare and childcare services.

Notes and Conclusions

There is an experimental edge contained in each of the films, even the more realist dramas. In the upside down training scene from Something Different I feel as if we see the vein of the creative idiosyncrasies we get in Daisies; which, in turn, isn't totally lost in the plainer Panelstory as through the sort of meditative interstitials of of the concrete panels being assembled into apartment blocks, we get a reminder of the sort of scrap book montage art-style that Daisies uses to a much more radical degree.

At first it is hard to track how one director could make such seemingly disparate films, but nonetheless there are very distinct commonalities: chiefly being, portraying the experience of young women within this particular period of time (60s/70s) in this particular place on Earth (Czechoslovakia) likely then reflecting Chytilová's own experiences and as such we seen her interesting sensibilities come through on screen.
