W032: Indefinite Hiatus

Week starting: 2021-04-26

Walking the Streets of Moscow (1967)

A nice light-hearted comedy drama mainly following three Muscovite youths and one Siberian youth passing through Moscow and the antics they get up to in a day. Its plot is as listless as its characters but that's not so important when it has as much charm and optimism as it does.

The Color of Pomegranates (1969)

A very visually striking film but it otherwise escapes any intelligent dicussion from me. I really don't know enough about traditional Armenian culture or the film's biographic subject to make much of a comment about it in these regards. Its images are really quite arresting though which makes up for the lack of any apparent narrative. I can imagine people who like stories hating this, but people who like more visual art mediums (painting, photography) loving it. I'm moreso in the middle but have great respect for it.

Unbelievable Adventures of Italians in Russia (1974)

A Rat Race (2001) style hunt for hidden treasure burried somewhere in Moscow featuring a traffic law obiding lion, a magic flying cigar and lots of well choreographed stunts. Not always laugh out loud funny but it definitely has its moments.

Notes and Conclusions

Since taking up full-time employment over the past three weeks, it has been a lot harder to crank out a post before the end of each Sunday about three films I have watched throughout the week. As such, I am reading much less than I would like to be and therefore the project is going on hold. I have found it great fun exploring films both niche and world famous from the earlier two thirds of cinema's history. Less-so feeling an eagerness to watch new films, I am more excited in the months ahead to watching films at a much slower pace. I really think it's possible that I have been spoiling myself with watching so many good films in such quick succession.

Nothing really connects these films other than that they were made in the Soviet Union. I only chose them as three that may make up a decent final set of films that I could watch all of and write about in a day. I'm thinking: when (or if) I return to this project I will maybe change the rules so that I can rewatch films I have already seen, so long as I haven't seen it for the blog. Till then.
