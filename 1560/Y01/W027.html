W027: Dorothy Arzner

Week starting: 2021-03-22

The Bride Wore Red (1937)

Joan Crawford is great as Anni as she navigates the high society world she has been thrown into. I like the dialogue style a lot and it does the heavy lifting in transporting you to '30s Trieste or The Alps. The direction is strong too and does a good job of moving the story at a nice pace while also considering such topics as class and economic mobility and the evasiveness of desire.

Dance Girl Dance (1940)

Lucille Ball and Maureen O'Hara are great and really are the driving force of the film. It's at times very funny but in a way that doesn't feel too outdated. I'm sure plenty could be read into it about its relevence today in evaluating how seriously women are taken in entertainment and art; but the enjoyment of the film doesn't require necessarily keeping this in mind. It very much stands for itself and makes for fun viewing.

First Comes Courage (1943)

Merle Oberon is great in this WW2 Norwegian resistance story. It's well put together and high octane for the time. The final act is some first rate edge-of-your-seat stuff and really brings to a head what is established earlier on.

Notes and Conclusions

The diversity of stories presented this week show off the skills of an effective director, in that while each of them are very different in narrative and theme they are each still eminently watchable and hold up well. These are Arzner's final three directorial credits but I would be interested in watching some of her other work should I be able to find them.
