W006: Courtroom Dramas

Week starting: 2020-10-26

12 Angry Men (1957)

What I like best about 12 Angry Men is it's simplicity and emersive quality. By most of it taking place in 'real-time' and with camera angles at the sitting hight of the juror's table, it feels almost as if you are the 13th Angry Man, making the writing and performances even more compelling, as you try to determine for yourself the defendant's guilt.

Anatomy of a Murder (1959)

Anatomy of a Murder's main merit is its ability to blend a certain amount of humour and Jazz with the grim nature of the court case. It's fun to watch Jimmy Steward's Paul Biegler bend the rules of engagement in the courtroom in order to help his client beat the case, even when he is charged with the murder of his wife's rapist.

Kramer Vs. Kramer (1979)

What immediately stands out to me about Kramer Vs. Kramer is how similar it is to 2019's Marriage Story. I enjoyed both films quite a bit - I think for the same reasons, but I can't quite pin down what exactly those reasons are. It's a devestating situation that is portrayed in both films and they both do a great job of translating those stories to film in a way that is accessible to people who haven't been through anything like it. Hoffman and Streep give killer, 'Academy Award Winning' performances and are reason enough to watch the film.

Notes and Conclusions

While these are films that do, in a major way, concern the American legal system, and to that end: 'The Courtroom', it is interesting to note how in each film the courtroom itself is really only a superficial framing to allow the drama to unfold. I'm sure if I watched more courtroom dramas that this property holds for most of them - I can't imagine a story about the courtroom in-and-of-itself being that interesting - but it was something particularly noticable in this week's films as I questioned whether each of these films were even very appropriate to viewed under the theme. 12 Angry Men only spends the first few minutes in the courtroom, and Anatomy of a Murder and Kramer Vs. Kramer don't get there until after the first hour.
