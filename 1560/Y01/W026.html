W026: John Carpenter

Week starting: 2021-03-15

Dark Star (1974)

Featuring some very inventive low budget SFX, Dark Star is, at times, very funny and generally is as effective as a satire of 2001 as Spaceballs is of Star Wars, if less flashy and outwardly silly.

Assault on Precinct 13 (1976)

Textbook John Carpenter. If it's not his best film, then it's definitely the one with the best soundtrack!

Eyes of Laura Mars (1978)

Faye Dunaway steals the show in this one, and fair enough! She's definitely got the chops to carry the the story and the film only really drags in the scenes in which she is not in. Carpenter should be commended for his skills as a screen-writer and story teller and while this one lacks his usual playful style and music (he didn't direct or score it) it still ends up being a half-way decent premonition thriller film in a similar sort of vein as Final Destination or Minority Report. What should you do when you foresee someone's death?

I only really inlcuded it this week as I've already seen all the other major Carpenter films pre-'81. It features a decent original song, however.

Notes and Conclusions

Knapp's cinematography and Carptenter's music featured in the first two films this week are what set them appart from Laura Mars and consequently makes this week's third film feel much less exciting in comparison, despite it's ambition.
