W030: Ingrid Bergman

Week starting: 2021-04-12

Spellbound (1945)

Maybe a more obscure Hitchcock (not that there are any really obscure Hitchcock films) that I likely would have not otherwise have seen without this week's theme (I'm saving Notorious - the other film Bergman did with Hitchcock - for another trilogy of Hitchcock's spies.)

Stromboli (1950)

Traditional and modern cultures clash in this film set in post-war Italy (... so what does the volcano represent?) The tuna fishing scene stands out as particularly gruesome yet nonetheless very memorable.

Autumn Sonata (1979)

Bergman directs Bergman! This week's stand out film. It's brilliant. Ullman and Ingrid Bergman play off each other wonderfully and they make it really easy to buy into their characters.

Notes and Conclusions

This week's conceit was nothing more tenuous than the fact that Ingrid Bergman shares a last name with Ingmar Bergman despite not being related. This minor connection allowed me to jump into the work of Bergman (Ingrid) - who I had heard a lot about but never seen on screen - in some films I knew nothing about. This finding small connections between films I have seen with other I have not and even smaller connections sometimes between three films which form a theatic trilogy of films I haven't seen has been the most pleasurable part of the project. This has let me go in 'blind' to many films I likely would have never seen or al least would have not seen without beforehand learning a lot about. Seeing a film without knowing anything about it is in my view the best way to watch any film - and these three opitimse the pleasure gained in trying to do this.

Bergman is a great talent, giving three great performances in three languages too! Who else can say that?!
