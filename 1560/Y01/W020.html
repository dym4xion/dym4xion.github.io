Week starting: 2021-02-01

Notes and Conclusions

Two firsts for the blog this week: (1) a back-to-back repition of of a single theme; (2) a week whereby I really don't have anything of note to say about each film on their own. They are each good films in their own right and perhaps if I had only to write about one of them I could give better thought-out view points. Something about watching these films next to each other and seeing the same techniques iterated over in slightly different ways has worked in such a way that they are very enjoyable to watch but that I struggle to point out anything significant about any one. When I look at the notes I took while watching each one I notice that they are generally quite superficial, but I think I can have a go at briefly giving a quick statement on why each stands out in at least one way.

TFOGTOR sticks in my head mostly for one of its later scenes where the couple make the namesake meal together late at night. Some really nice Jeanne Dielman level commitment to realism without any of the uneasiness. Floating Weeds is interesting to see in relation to the other films I've seen that look at the unexpected behind-the-scenes stories that happen in theater production (The Producers (1967), Birdman (2014)) but still with the stuff that makes an Ozu film great. Good Morning is my favourite for the week and maybe my favourite Ozu so far for how brilliantly it balances silliness and sentimentality.

The original conceit for this week was to watch three Ozu films without 'Tokyo' or the name of a season in the title. By coincidence, it would seem that this has had the effect of offering a different perspective on Ozu. These films feel much less meditative or reflective than last week's three and are a more energetic, though no less 'real' feeling, portrayals of midcentury life in Japan. It might be of note to some that the seccond two from this week are self-remakes of silent films Ozu made in the 30s - an interesting practice I had not seen anywhere in film production before but would seem like something that is not totally unconventional.
