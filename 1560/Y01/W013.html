W013: Ernst Lubitsch

Week starting: 2020-12-14

Trouble in Paradise (1932)

Trouble in Paradise's direction is strikingly modern for a film that is 88 years old. The ending is equal parts tragic and ecstatic. Strong performances from leads and supporting cast.

Ninotchka (1939)

Featuring a litany of decent performances, the best of which coming from the three wacky, luxury seeking commisars, Ninotchka feels both (sometimes at the same time) cynical and lighthearted. Cynical towards its characters who seem to have strongly held convictions, and lightearted about their willingness or tendency to change.

If it really wanted to be a smart satire it would be able to make fun of both state ideolodies at the same time or at least have a better sense of the tragic like 2017's The Death of Stalin. Something about it's use in the 1947 Italian elections would suggest its less-than-honest-to-goodness filmmaking intentions. You would have to be a staunch individualist to truly love it.

The Shop Around the Corner (1940)

Jimmy Stewart is as competant as ever in one of his earliest performances I have seen him in, The Shop Around the Corner also features an equally strong performance from his co-star Margaret Sullavan. While the two take center stage, that shouldn't detract from the really exceptional interpersonal dynamics between the shop's employees which is what really sets the film apart. It's a warm and saccarine but far less ideologically on-the-nose film than Ninotchka and more honorable than Trouble in Paradise.

The manipulation of Novak by Kralik as he realises first their mutual coincidence is, of course by modern standards, unfashionable and uncomfortable but it does a decent enough job of evading being totally sadistic. Unintentionally (on my part), it also functions somewhat as a Christmas film so if you are looking for one to watch then this is one.

Notes and Conclusions

All three are perfectly enjoyable films. Trouble in Paradise and The Shop Around the Corner stand out for me, Ninotchka lacks the subtlety of the other two.
