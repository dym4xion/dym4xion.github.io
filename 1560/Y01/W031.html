W031: Judy Garland

Week starting: 2021-04-19

Meet Me in St. Louis (1944)

Perhaps the most glorious of the glorious Technicolor films I have seen. But I am yet to see The Wizard of Oz in full HD. Lighthearted and fun, like all good musicals it doesn't let the songs get in the way of the story.

Judgement at Nuremberg (1961)

Several great performances from a great cast. It's about three hours long but it doesn't feel like it - the court sessions breeze by.

Gay Purr-ee (1962)

Great colours, great animation. Interesting love-letter to the French impressionist movement.

Notes and Conclusions

Garland is great in each of these. While she isn't front and center in Judgement at Nuremberg she nonetheless gives a great dramatic performances. Her singing in the first and last of this week's films is also great and provided a fun change of pace.
