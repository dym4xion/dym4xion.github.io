About The 1560 Project

The 1560 Project aims to celebrate older films. For the next ten years I aim to watch three films per week, each of which being made before 1981 (a total of 10 * 3 * 52 = 1560 films).

1980 being the inclusive cut-off point was chosen somewhat arbitrarily. A compromise between the most amount of decades of film I was willing to sacrifice while still allowing for talking about some of the greatest feature classics that I have yet to see. I could have chosen to abstain from the past 50 years of film by making the cut-off 1970 but there is something I find so interesting about films from the '70s (The Texas Chainsaw Massacre, Alien, The Warriors, Network, Jaws, The Wicker Man, Westworld) that I enjoy so much that I wouldn't want to miss out on film's from that decade that I am still eager to see (Suspiria, The Deer Hunter, Kramer vs. Kramer, Stalker). I could have had the project focus on films from the whole of the 20th century, by making the cut-off point 2000, but I felt that this wouldn't have presented much of a challenge at all.

The project will operate under a set of rules that will help to fully realise the aims of the project. These rules are as follows:

  1. All films must have been released before 1981.
  2. No film must be watched more than once.
  3. Films that have been seen before the start of the project cannot be seen again, even if they fit the criteria.
  4. The three films watched each week must each exist within a common (however tenuous) theme.
  5. Themes may be repeated when appropriate.
  6. No more than 1000 words may be written each week about why the films are good and why/if they work well as a thematic trilogy.
  7. Each film must have a runtime of at least 40 minutes.
