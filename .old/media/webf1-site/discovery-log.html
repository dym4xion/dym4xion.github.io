  • Evaluated and justified what we thought were the most and least useful websites as well as produce an idea for a website that could be created "if money and status were no object" to provide a currently non-existant service. Submitted responses:

Most useful site: google.com

Provides a centralised engine to quickly access the majority of services that I most frequently use. Advanced search option allows for a refined search for more specific sites. Majority of searches are satisfied within the first two pages of results. Additionally, creating an account with Google provides instant access to a litany of other google services as well as gaining the ability to sign into third party websites with google login details, making for a more streamlined experience and centralised account security, limiting the distribution of sensitive information.

Most flawed site: facebook.com

Promoting an attitude of being liberal with the quantity and type of information people should make public could lead to an increase in personal data vulnerability. Creates a pathway for social engineers posing as mutual friends to gain access to personal information. Unnecessarily intrusive data collection when generating adverts. Provides an effective method for communicating with coworkers, friends and relatives due to large user base but could be done in a manner that minimises the publicity of personal details (liked pages, friends/family lists).

Website Idea:

A project/idea development service whereby users could publicly submit ideas and projects they want to complete to gain the attention of potential collaborators. Projects can be of any scale and can be based around any technical specialty or skill set. Users can host a portfolio on a personal page for project initiators to evaluate whether other users who request to collaborate on a project would be appropriate. Project initiator would describe the project they are undertaking and list the required skills of individuals they are looking to collaborate with. Project development environment could be hosted in browser with collaborators having access to a file repository, text and voice communication.

While there are online project development services and file repositories, many are directed to software development. This site could aggregate projects based around anything from creative writing to mechanical engineering, to enable users to take part in and develop ideas in their field.

The Impact of the Ruby Programming Language on Modern Computing:

Perhaps Ruby’s most significant impact on modern computing is through its implementation on the Ruby on Rails web framework, which gained the programming language popularity with programmers outside of Japan due to Rails’ “Convention over Configuration” philosophy which allowed development of a web-service to be intuitive as well as many users finding site maintenance easier (Marx, 2014). This philosophy led to Ruby on Rails to be an effective web framework against other web frameworks, with sites such as: Hulu, Yellow Pages and GitHub being developed on Rails (Wojciechowicz, 2015). Rails has been able to maintain its popularity due to users finding Roby code to be highly readable and having a focus on being a completely object-oriented language which has enabled developers to work productively while also making the Rails frameworks require less effort to learn. As well as this, Ruby gains favorability with web developers from the extensive amount of open source code made available by the Rails community, as well as the majority of Rails’ libraries being open source, removing any licensing costs for developers (Ford, 2014). Ruby continues to develop as a programming language through the support it gets from its community. With ‘Ruby User Groups’, ‘Mailing Lists and Newsgroups’, blogs, conferences and a GitHub repository; avid Ruby users are able to contribute to the improvement of the language while also being able to gain support and advice on their own projects (Ruby Community Page, 2017).

These improvements will likely have an improvement on following work as it encourages a more professional syntax of writing which prioritises clarity and impersonality.
